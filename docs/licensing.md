# Licensing

GB-STAMP is released under **the most open terms compatible with the sources it builds on** — with one firm requirement: **any re-use must credit Stephen Gadd and the World Historical Gazetteer.**

## The short version

| Part | Licence |
|---|---|
| **Source code** (`*.py`, scripts, tooling) | **MIT** |
| **Data** (the GB-STAMP typed gazetteer and derived files) | **CC-BY-SA 4.0** |
| **Documentation** (`docs/`, this site) | **CC-BY-SA 4.0** |

## Why CC-BY-SA for the data (and not something more permissive)

The dataset is a **derivative of GB1900**, which is published under **CC-BY-SA**. Share-alike is "sticky": any dataset that incorporates GB1900 must itself be shared under the same (or a compatible) share-alike licence. **CC-BY-SA 4.0 is therefore the *most open* licence we are permitted to use for the data** — we cannot drop the share-alike condition, and we have not added any further restriction.

The **code** does not incorporate GB1900 data, so it is offered under the more permissive **MIT** licence.

## Required attribution

Any re-use of the GB-STAMP data or documentation must credit:

> **GB-STAMP** — Stephen Gadd / World Historical Gazetteer, derived from **GB1900** (Great Britain Historical GIS / A Vision of Britain through Time; CC-BY-SA) and the **Ordnance Survey Characteristic Sheets** (National Library of Scotland; CC-BY), with feature types from the Getty **Art & Architecture Thesaurus** (ODC-BY).

and must preserve the **CC-BY-SA 4.0** share-alike terms on any redistribution.

## Upstream sources and their terms

- **GB1900** — CC-BY-SA. Attribution + share-alike propagate to this dataset.
- **Ordnance Survey six-inch County Series maps** — the maps themselves are out of Crown copyright; the **scans and Characteristic-Sheet reproductions** are provided by the **National Library of Scotland** under CC-BY.
- **Getty Art & Architecture Thesaurus (AAT)** — [ODC Attribution License](https://www.getty.edu/research/tools/vocabularies/license.html).
