# Licensing

GB-STAMP is released under **the most open terms compatible with the sources it builds on** — with one firm requirement: **any re-use must credit Stephen Gadd and the World Historical Gazetteer.**

## The short version

| Part | Licence |
|---|---|
| **Source code** (`*.py`, scripts, tooling) | **MIT** |
| **Data** (the GB-STAMP typed gazetteer and derived files) | **CC-BY 4.0** |
| **Documentation** (`docs/`, this site) | **CC-BY 4.0** |

## Why CC-BY 4.0

GB-STAMP takes its raw text and coordinates from the **GB1900 raw dump**, which is released under **CC0** (public-domain dedication) — so there is **no share-alike obligation** to propagate. The only attribution-bearing upstreams are the **Ordnance Survey Characteristic Sheets** (via the National Library of Scotland, CC-BY) and the Getty **Art & Architecture Thesaurus** (ODC-BY), both of which require attribution but not share-alike. **CC-BY 4.0 is therefore the most open licence we can use** while honouring those sources — and while ensuring GB-STAMP itself is properly credited.

The **code** is offered under the more permissive **MIT** licence.

## Required attribution

Any re-use of the GB-STAMP data or documentation must credit:

> **GB-STAMP** — Stephen Gadd / World Historical Gazetteer, derived from **GB1900** (Great Britain Historical GIS / A Vision of Britain through Time; raw dump CC0) and the **Ordnance Survey Characteristic Sheets** (National Library of Scotland; CC-BY), with feature types from the Getty **Art & Architecture Thesaurus** (ODC-BY).

## Upstream sources and their terms

- **GB1900 raw dump** — CC0. No restrictions; attribution is a courtesy, not a requirement of that source.
- **Ordnance Survey six-inch County Series maps** — the maps themselves are out of Crown copyright; the **scans and Characteristic-Sheet reproductions** are provided by the **National Library of Scotland** under CC-BY.
- **Getty Art & Architecture Thesaurus (AAT)** — [ODC Attribution License](https://www.getty.edu/research/tools/vocabularies/license.html).
